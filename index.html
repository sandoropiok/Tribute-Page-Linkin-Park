LINKIN PARK

Linkin Park is among both the best-selling bands of the 21st century.

Linkin Park is an American rock band formed in Agoura Hills, California, in 1996.

History

Linkin Park was founded by three high school friends: Mike Shinoda, Rob Bourdon, and Brad Delson. The three attended Agoura High School in Agoura Hills, California, a suburb of Los Angeles...

Legacy

Linkin Park has sold more than 100 million records worldwide. The group's first studio album Hybrid Theory is one of the best-selling albums in the US (12 million copies shipped) and worldwide (30 million copies sold). Billboard estimates that Linkin Park earned US$5 million between May 2011 and May 2012, making them the 40th-highest-paid musical artist. 11 of the band's singles have reached the number one position on Billboard's Alternative Songs chart, the second-most for any artist.[

Musical style and influences

Linkin Park combines elements of metal, industrial, punk, pop, electronic and hip hop.More specifically, the band has been categorized as alternative rock, nu metal, rap rock, rap metal, alternative metal, electronic rock, pop rock, hard rock, and industrial rock. Despite being considered nu metal, the band never considered themselves as such.

Many of the group's song lyrics deal with alienation, introversion, and personal struggle and suffering.
